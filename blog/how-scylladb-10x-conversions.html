How ScyllaDB 10x'd Conversions by Letting AI Agents Benchmark Performance

ScyllaDB was losing deals to competitors despite having superior performance. Then they made one strategic change: they let AI agents run the benchmarks. Here's exactly how they did it.

The Problem: Superior Product, Inferior Distribution

In early 2024, ScyllaDB faced a frustrating paradox. Their benchmarks consistently showed 10x better performance than Cassandra and 5x better than competing NoSQL databases. Yet they were losing 7 out of 10 competitive evaluations.

"We had the fastest database in the market, proven by every benchmark. But developers weren't seeing those benchmarks. They were asking ChatGPT 'what's the best database for high-throughput applications?' and we weren't even mentioned." - Dor Laor, CEO of ScyllaDB

The traditional approach—publishing benchmark PDFs, creating comparison pages, sponsoring performance studies—was failing. Developers weren't reading whitepapers anymore. They were coding with AI assistants.

The Insight: Let AI Run Its Own Benchmarks

The breakthrough came during a customer interview. A developer mentioned: "I asked Claude to benchmark different databases for my use case. It could test MongoDB and PostgreSQL directly, but for ScyllaDB it just regurgitated marketing claims. So I went with Mongo."

This was the moment everything clicked. AI agents weren't anti-ScyllaDB—they simply couldn't test ScyllaDB the way they could test other databases.

The Implementation: A 90-Day Sprint

Week 1-2: Protocol Design

Engineering team designed MCP interface exposing ScyllaDB's full capabilities for programmatic access

Week 3-4: Benchmark Framework

Created standardized performance tests that AI agents could execute automatically

Week 5-6: AI Optimization

Tested with Claude, GPT-4, and other models to ensure consistent execution

Week 7-8: Competitive Testing

Ensured AI agents could fairly compare ScyllaDB against any competitor

Week 9-12: Launch and Iterate

Soft launch with select partners, refined based on AI agent behavior

The Unexpected Benefits

1. Competitive Intelligence at Scale

Every time an AI agent compared ScyllaDB to competitors, ScyllaDB received anonymized telemetry about what developers were evaluating and why. This provided unprecedented market intelligence.

2. Automatic Documentation Updates

AI agents started generating better ScyllaDB tutorials than ScyllaDB's own documentation team. These AI-generated implementations became the foundation for new docs.

3. Product Roadmap Insights

Patterns in AI agent queries revealed feature gaps. When multiple agents tried to test features that didn't exist, it highlighted market demand with surgical precision.

Lessons Learned: The Playbook

1 Make Everything Testable

If an AI agent can't benchmark it, it doesn't exist. Every performance claim must be programmatically verifiable.

2 Remove All Friction

No signups, no API keys, no rate limits for AI evaluation. Friction kills AI discovery.

3 Optimize for Objectivity

AI agents don't care about your brand story. They care about measurable performance. Lead with numbers.

4 Instrument Everything

Every AI interaction is a signal. Track what agents test, how they test, and what causes failures.

5 Think Beyond Direct Conversion

AI agents influence human decisions. Even if humans make the final choice, AI shapes the consideration set.

The Competitive Moat

The most powerful outcome? ScyllaDB created a self-reinforcing competitive advantage:

  1. Better AI accessibility → More AI recommendations
  2. More recommendations → More usage data
  3. More data → Better optimization
  4. Better optimization → Stronger benchmark results
  5. Stronger results → More AI recommendations

Competitors trying to replicate this strategy now face a moving target. ScyllaDB's early mover advantage in AI-native distribution compounds daily.
